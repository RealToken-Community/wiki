Voting power is established from the REGs owned, regardless of:

  • the way they are owned: on its Wallet, deposited on a Liquidity Pool or deposited on the Incentive Vault,
  • the blockchain used (Gnosis, Polygon or Ethereum).

At an unpredictable date (so that there is no manipulation), RealT takes a snapshot to collect the situation of all the REGs. From this collection, a program (offChain) calculates the Voting Power of each. This is a weighting of the mode of use of the REGs (and the counterparts in the liquidity pools) which is decided by the DAO.

The quantity of Voting Power token of each remains unchanged, until the next snapshot (even if the quantity of REG has moved in the meantime). This token is non-transferable. The quantity of Voting Power of a wallet is visible on the application Tally, or  explorer.

Weighting algorithm

Version 1

The first snapshots used the following weighting:

  • 1 REG in a Wallet or the Incentive Vault is worth 1 PowerVoting
  • 1 REG in a liquidity pool is worth 1.2 PowerVoting,
  • The stablecoins brought in return in the Liquidity Pool are worth 0.5 PowerVoting of the REG equivalent

This first algorithm was established by RealT. The following will be voted on by the DAO: 

- Vote to create the Committee dedicated to the study of a new algorithm: [RIP00007], 
- Committee discussions visible (read-only) on this Telegram 
- Vote for a new algorithm: [RIP00018]. 

Version 2

 

The changes are as follows : 

  • New weighting, based on REG usage (see image).
  • REG amounts are the average of REG held since the previous snapshot.
  • Voting Powers <1 will be considered null.

They are applied from RIP00022.

Calculation program

The program, created by RealT, for the snapshot and calculation is available: Github
Its operation is detailed in chapter 4.4 of this page

For each snapshot, RealT generates two types of files (in csv and json format):

  • balancesREG: Lists all wallets that have REG, with the quantity for each mode of use,
  • powerVotingREG_balances: List of wallets that have PowerVoting with the quantity resulting from the calculation by the algorithm. The following addresses are excluded from this second list (because they cannot vote): Liquidity Pools, Incentive Vaults, Safes that manage the DAO budgets, and the allocation to Mt Pelerin.
